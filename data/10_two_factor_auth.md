
# Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) adds an extra layer of security to your CloudSuite account. Even if someone knows your password, they won't be able to access your account without the second verification step.

CloudSuite recommends enabling 2FA for all users. It is required for Enterprise administrators.

## Available 2FA Methods

You can choose one of the following options:

### Authenticator App (Recommended)

Supported apps include:

* Google Authenticator
* Authy
* Microsoft Authenticator
* Any app that supports TOTP codes

### SMS Verification

A verification code is sent to your registered phone number.

Availability may vary depending on your country and mobile carrier.

### Security Keys

Enterprise customers can use hardware security keys that support WebAuthn or FIDO2.

## Setting Up 2FA

To enable Two-Factor Authentication:

1. Open  **Account Settings** .
2. Select  **Security** .
3. Click  **Two-Factor Authentication** .
4. Choose your preferred verification method.
5. Follow the setup instructions.
6. Confirm the verification code.
7. Save your changes.

If using an authenticator app, scan the QR code displayed during setup.

## Backup Codes

After enabling 2FA, CloudSuite generates backup codes.

Store these codes in a safe place.

Backup codes can be used to sign in if you lose access to your phone or authenticator app.

Each backup code can only be used once.

## Lost Access to Your Device?

If you still have backup codes:

1. Select **Use a Backup Code** on the login screen.
2. Enter one of your saved codes.
3. Sign in and update your 2FA settings if needed.

If you no longer have access to your device or backup codes, contact Support for identity verification.

Additional verification may be required before access can be restored.

## Disabling 2FA

To disable Two-Factor Authentication:

1. Open  **Account Settings** .
2. Go to  **Security** .
3. Select  **Disable Two-Factor Authentication** .
4. Enter your password.
5. Complete the current 2FA verification step.

This helps prevent unauthorized changes to your account security settings.
