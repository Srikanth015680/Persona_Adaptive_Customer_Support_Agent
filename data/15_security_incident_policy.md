
# Security Incident Reporting

If you believe your CloudSuite account or data may have been compromised, report the issue as soon as possible.

Security reports are reviewed with high priority to help protect customer accounts and information.

## Examples of Security Incidents

You should report any of the following:

* Unauthorized access to your account
* A lost or stolen device that was signed in to CloudSuite
* Suspicious emails pretending to be from CloudSuite
* Unexpected account activity
* A potential security vulnerability in the platform

## What You Should Do First

If you suspect a security issue:

1. Change your password immediately.
2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) if it is not already enabled.
3. Review your active sessions and sign out of any devices you do not recognize.
4. Report the issue to the CloudSuite security team.

Taking these steps quickly can help prevent unauthorized access.

## Reporting a Security Issue

To report an issue:

1. Open  **Help** .
2. Select  **Report a Security Issue** .
3. Provide as much information as possible about the incident.

Helpful information includes:

* When the issue was noticed
* Any suspicious activity observed
* Screenshots or error messages
* Device and browser information

## Account Access Problems

If someone has changed your password, email address, or authentication settings, you may not be able to recover the account through normal self-service tools.

In these situations, additional identity verification may be required before access can be restored.

## Reporting Security Vulnerabilities

Security researchers and customers can report suspected vulnerabilities directly to the CloudSuite security team.

When reporting an issue, include:

* Steps to reproduce the problem
* Screenshots or supporting evidence
* Any potential impact you observed

The security team reviews all reports and investigates confirmed issues.
