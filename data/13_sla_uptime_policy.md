
# SLA and Uptime Policy

CloudSuite works to provide a reliable and available service for all customers.

This article explains uptime commitments, how downtime is measured, and how to request service credits when applicable.

## Uptime Targets

| Plan       | Uptime Target        | Service Credits               |
| ---------- | -------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Starter    | No guaranteed SLA    | Not available                 |
| Pro        | 99.5% monthly uptime | Eligible for service credits  |
| Enterprise | 99.9% monthly uptime | Eligible under contract terms |

Enterprise customers may have custom SLA agreements based on their subscription contract.

## What Counts as Downtime?

Downtime occurs when the CloudSuite application or API is unavailable to users due to a service issue.

Examples include:

* Application not loading
* API requests returning server errors
* Users unable to access core platform features

Scheduled maintenance periods do not count as downtime.

Maintenance notifications are announced in advance whenever possible.

## Checking Service Status

Current and historical service status information is available on the CloudSuite status page.

The status page includes:

* Active incidents
* Scheduled maintenance notices
* Recent service disruptions
* Resolution updates

Customers can use the status page to confirm whether an issue is related to a known platform incident.

## Requesting Service Credits

If an outage affects your account and your plan includes SLA coverage:

1. Review the incident on the status page.
2. Gather any relevant incident information.
3. Submit a request through  **Billing → SLA Credits** .
4. Include the incident reference if available.

Requests should be submitted within 30 days of the affected incident.

## Enterprise Support During Incidents

Enterprise customers may receive additional updates during major service disruptions.

These updates can include:

* Affected services
* Incident duration
* Recovery progress
* Follow-up summaries after resolution
